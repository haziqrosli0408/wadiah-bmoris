Proposed Project Title 
Provide a concise and clear project title that reflects the core objective of your project. 
BMoris – Bahasa Melayu Speech Assistant application that teaches how to pronounce the Malay 
language. 
Project Overview 
Briefly describe the problem your project aims to solve, the objectives of the project, and the 
significance of the work. 
"Bahasa Melayu Speech Assistant" (BMoris) combines AI speech recognition, adaptive feedback, 
and gamified learning to help learners improve Malay pronunciation, grammar, and their 
confidence. Designed for non-native Malaysians, foreign learners, and adult second-language 
learners in Malaysia, the app utilizes interactive voice recognition and guided practice to provide 
users with real-time feedback, enabling them to naturally improve and correct errors. BMoris 
can be used by schools, self-learners, and professionals to improve Malay pronunciation 
anytime, anywhere because of its flexibility, offline mode and AI driven design, making it suitable 
for both academic and personal learning contexts. The learning content in BMoris is structured 
based on the International Malay Communication Module, developed under the guidance of 
Madam Wardatul Aisyah, the Head of the International Section at Universiti Kuala Lumpur 
(MIIT).

Problem Statements 
What specific problems does your project aim to address? 
1. Difficulty Recognising and Correcting Pronunciation Mistakes Leads to Low Confidence 
Learners frequently practise speaking without real-time correction, reinforcing incorrect 
pronunciation patterns. Nowawi and Ahmad (2023) observed that when learners used interactive 
Malay language applications, they were able to practise pronunciation more effectively and gained 
confidence compared to traditional classroom-only approaches. Similarly, Lo et al. (2025) found 
that Duolingo’s features such as “Listen and Repeat” exercises helped students model native 
pronunciation, which directly improved their speaking skills and boosted their confidence in 
communication. The lack of regular guided practice causes mistakes to persist over time and leads 
to low confidence when speaking Bahasa Melayu, as learners are afraid of being misunderstood 
or making mistakes in real-life conversations. 
2. Difficulty in Tracking and Assessing Pronunciation Progress 
Tracking the improvement of Bahasa Melayu pronunciation is challenging without an efficient tool 
to record and evaluate speech. Learners and instructors alike face difficulties in assessing whether 
the learner's pronunciation is improving, where the weaknesses lie, and what specific areas need 
more practice. This makes it hard to implement personalized learning strategies or monitor 
progress effectively. Hawamdeh and Soykan (2021) highlighted that mobile technologies allow 
teachers and students to systematically monitor language progress through features like quizzes 
and analytics, which are difficult to achieve with traditional methods. In line with this, Jumadi et 
al. (2024) argued that artificial intelligence can provide adaptive feedback and performance 
tracking, helping educators identify weak areas and tailor instruction more effectively. 
3. Limited Access to Personalized Language Learning Tools 
Many Bahasa Melayu learners, especially non-native speakers, lack access to language resources 
that provide them with individualised pronunciation feedback. Jumadi et al. (2024) emphasised 
that many existing language tools provide only general feedback, which is frustrating for learners 
with unique pronunciation challenges. They suggest that AI-driven tutoring systems can 
personalise the learning process by targeting individual needs. Chen, Chen, and Lin (2020) similarly 
noted that personalised and adaptive learning pathways supported by AI are essential to maximise 
student outcomes. Without these resources, learners are unable to concentrate on their individual 
pronunciation issues. Because general language learning tools do not cater to the needs of 
individual learners, particularly when it comes to mastering complex sounds and pronunciation 
patterns, this leads to frustration and slow progress.

4.  Lack of Exposure to Authentic Speaking Environments 
Learners usually have limited opportunities to practise Bahasa Melayu in natural, conversational 
contexts. Learners who have not been exposed to authentic dialogues, cultural expressions, or 
real-life circumstances may struggle to use correct pronunciation effectively during actual 
communication. Lo et al. (2025) acknowledged that while Duolingo supports pronunciation and 
listening practice, it still falls short in providing authentic conversational settings, which are 
essential for real-world communication. On the other hand, Nowawi and Ahmad (2023) showed 
that interactive multimedia apps for Malay learning expose learners to cultural expressions and 
natural contexts, helping them apply language skills more effectively outside the classroom.

Proposed Solution 
Outline the solution you propose to implement. Include any technology stack (tools, 
programming languages & frameworks), and methodologies that will be used. 
Learners find it challenging to pronounce Malay words correctly and confidently, especially those 
unfamiliar with the language’s unique phonemes, such as ng, ny, and kh. To address these 
challenges, this project proposes BMoris — an AI-powered Malay learning platform that is 
available on both mobile and web platforms. The system integrates advanced technologies to 
enhance language learning through: 
1. Real-time pronunciation feedback with phoneme-level scoring. 
2. A bilingual AI tutor for conversation and translation between Malay and English. 
3. Adaptive learning which automatically adjusts lesson difficulty based on learner performance. 
4. Gamified learning features such as experience points (XP), streaks, badges, and leaderboards. 
5. Additional support for offline access and accessibility features, ensuring inclusivity for 
all learners. 
We use OpenAI (ChatGPT) as our main AI model to power conversation and language 
understanding in BMoris. The effectiveness of the app will be evaluated through regular user 
satisfaction surveys to measure how well it meets users’ needs and expectations, while 
continuous feedback will be collected through an in-app feedback system that allows users to 
share comments, report issues, and suggest improvements directly. BMoris ensures accuracy by 
using verified content from the MIIT syllabus, expert review, and controlled AI prompts. Ethical 
measures include data privacy, user consent, cultural sensitivity, and transparency in AI
generated responses.  
The app will provide interactive assessments such as quizzes, pronunciation tests, and practice 
exercises to measure user progress. User performance will be tracked automatically through 
scores and completion rates, and displayed on a personal dashboard showing their progress, 
achievements, and areas that need improvement.

Methodology – Agile Methodology (Scrum) 
We used Agile Scrum to manage our project development in short sprints, ensuring continuous 
progress and feedback. Meanwhile, our architecture follows a DevOps approach - integrating 
code, testing, deployment, and monitoring — so the BMoris app can be updated quickly and 
safely whenever we make improvements. 
The workflow is organized as follows: 
1. Sprint Planning 
Define key objectives and features for each sprint based on project priorities. 
2. Development Iterations 
Implement features incrementally, starting with the core system modules — authentication, ASR 
feedback, adaptive learning engine, and teacher dashboard. 
3. Testing, Feedback & Refinement 
Gather feedback from test users to evaluate usability, pronunciation accuracy, and learning 
experience. 
4. Final Optimization & Deployment 
Conduct final performance tuning, bug fixing, and accessibility checks. Ensure the platform is 
stable, secure, and optimized before deployment on both mobile and web platforms.
